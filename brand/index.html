Mark & wordmark

Atlas is a wireframe globe — an outline sphere with a vertical meridian, three equal-height latitude rings, and a thin solid shadow on each side. The rings pull in so their tips tuck just inside the shadows, so the whole thing reads as a globe curving into its own terminator.

Reversed · on ink
Favicon · heavier weight

The favicon uses thicker strokes and drops the finest detail so the globe survives at 16 px. Never shrink the full-detail mark below 28 px — use the favicon build instead.

The wordmark is set in Bowlby One SC — a fat, rounded uppercase — tracked in to −.03em. 02

Clear space & size

Keep clear space of at least ¼ of the mark's diameter on every side. Nothing — type, rules, edges — enters that zone.

Minimum size for the full mark is 28 px. Below that, switch to the favicon build.

03

Secondary marks

Six alternates share the same circle and monochrome rule. Each has one job — they extend the system without diluting the primary mark. Use one at a time, never mixed on the same surface.

Offset
Section dividers & "new issue" stamps. Its drift suits transitions.
Eclipse
Social avatar & share images. Cleanest solo circle at tight crops.
Graticule
Data & sources contexts — the appendix, "by the numbers."
Engraved hatch
Print & PDF headers, premium leave-behinds. Currency texture.
Halftone
Background texture or oversized watermark behind a hero.
Reticle
Loading / "tracking" states & live-data badges.

Graticule and Reticle carry the most fine detail — keep them at medium size and up. Eclipse and Offset handle the tiny slots. Live placements: Halftone waters the hero, Offset stamps each issue card, Reticle badges the masthead, Graticule marks the sources appendix, and Eclipse fronts the social share image.

07

Do & don't

Do

  • Let ink and paper carry the page; add color like seasoning.
  • Use one mark per surface, at or above its minimum size.
  • Keep the full clear-space zone around the mark empty.
  • Reserve teal for signal — active state, one link, one figure, the "How to act" flag.
  • Save red for the Subscribe badge, the "New" stamp, and genuine alerts.

Don't

  • Recolor the mark, add gradients, or drop shadows behind it.
  • Mix two secondary marks on the same surface.
  • Shrink the full-detail mark below 28 px — use the favicon.
  • Color headlines, body text, or multiple stats teal.
  • Scatter red — beyond the fixed Subscribe badge, keep to one red signal per page.
08

Voice

Sharp, clever, with heart. Atlas writes to a peer who runs paid media — an ad account manager or creative strategist — never down to a student. Full detail lives in brand-voice-guidelines.md.

The Brief · default

Unsigned, written to the reader in practical second person. About 90% of Atlas — the weekly items and takeaways.

The editorial "we" · rare

First person, reserved for a stated opinion or a piece of genuine admiration. Its power is in the scarcity.

The three-beat

What's happening → Why it matters → How to act. Every item moves in that rhythm — and the rhythm is the brand.

Sharp · clever · heart

Lead with the shift. One true, clever line per piece. Name the human stakes — warmth in the framing, never in exclamation points.

Do

  • Lead with the shift; cut the throat-clearing.
  • Let one clever, true line breathe per piece.
  • Name the human on the other side of the metric.
  • Reserve "we" for conviction; state opinions plainly.

Don't

  • Explain what a strategist already knows.
  • Force a pun or chase a trend past the signal bar.
  • Manufacture hot takes for engagement.
  • Use hype words, emoji, or exclamation points for warmth.
