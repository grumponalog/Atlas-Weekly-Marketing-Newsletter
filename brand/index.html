01

The mark

Atlas is a wireframe globe — an outline sphere with a vertical meridian, three equal-height latitude rings, and a thin solid shadow on each side. The rings pull in so their tips tuck just inside the shadows, so the whole thing reads as a globe curving into its own terminator.

Primary · on paper
Reversed · on ink
Favicon · heavier weight

The favicon uses thicker strokes and drops the finest detail so the globe survives at 16 px. Never shrink the full-detail mark below 28 px — use the favicon build instead.

02

Clear space & size

Keep clear space of at least ¼ of the mark's diameter on every side. Nothing — type, rules, edges — enters that zone.

16px favicon
28px min
60px masthead

Minimum size for the full mark is 28 px. Below that, switch to the favicon build.

03

Secondary marks

Six alternates share the same circle and monochrome rule. Each has one job — they extend the system without diluting the primary mark. Use one at a time, never mixed on the same surface.

Offset
Section dividers & "new issue" stamps. Its drift suits transitions.
Eclipse
Social avatar & share images. Cleanest solo circle at tight crops.
Graticule
Data & sources contexts — the appendix, "by the numbers."
Engraved hatch
Print & PDF headers, premium leave-behinds. Currency texture.
Halftone
Background texture or oversized watermark behind a hero.
Reticle
Loading / "tracking" states & live-data badges.

Graticule and Reticle carry the most fine detail — keep them at medium size and up. Eclipse and Offset handle the tiny slots.

Warm-vs-cool is the whole point: teal and red never share a job. Teal is the default signal; red only when something is genuinely urgent or brand-new. If a page needs more than one red thing, it needs a rethink, not more red.

07

Do & don't

Do

  • Let ink and paper carry the page; add color like seasoning.
  • Use one mark per surface, at or above its minimum size.
  • Keep the full clear-space zone around the mark empty.
  • Reserve teal for signal — active state, one link, one figure.
  • Save red for genuinely new or urgent moments.

Don't

  • Recolor the mark, add gradients, or drop shadows behind it.
  • Mix two secondary marks on the same surface.
  • Shrink the full-detail mark below 28 px — use the favicon.
  • Color headlines, body text, or multiple stats teal.
  • Use red for anything routine, or more than once per page.
