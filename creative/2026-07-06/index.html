The First Cut

The work worth stealing from this week — shown, with a short take on why each one lands.

This week's picks

Columbia Sportswear

Expedition Impossible

Goal
Dramatize how far the gear goes — a pure durability-and-belief flex.
Audience
Outdoor and everyday buyers who choose on trust in the product.
Result
Widely awarded and picked up in press.

Columbia offered its entire company — mannequins, coffee machine, a taxidermy beaver — to any flat-earther who could photograph the edge of the world. A brand so sure its gear survives the ends of the earth it'll wager the office on it.

Steal it Let the product's promise write the dare.

Club Deportivo Municipal · McCann Lima

The Thousand Sponsors of Muni

Goal
Fund a relegated club by democratizing shirt sponsorship.
Audience
Local businesses and fans priced out of traditional sponsorship.
Result
Cannes Entertainment Lions for Sport — Grand Prix 2026.

A cash-strapped Peruvian club cut its shirt into 1,000 pixel-grid sponsor squares from ~100 soles each, turning the kit into a neighborhood directory any local business could join.

Steal it Sometimes a thousand tiny doors beat one big one.

SAP · BBDO

Office Cat

Goal
Reframe business AI from hype to concrete, useful tasks.
Audience
Enterprise decision-makers wary of AI buzz.

SAP explains its business AI through a Van Gogh–styled cat that pads around an office naming the dull, useful things the software actually handles — an abstract enterprise capability made legible and even charming, the hardest trick in B2B.

Steal it When the product is invisible, borrow a character people already love.

See the work →

Pinterest · House of Creative (dir. Tim Godsall)

The P is for Performance

Goal
Reposition Pinterest as a lower-funnel, performance ad platform.
Audience
Advertisers and performance marketers.
Result
Cited +28% conversions and up to +96% traffic for advertisers.

Six grindhouse mini action films — deadpan heroines and a "High action! Lower funnel!" voiceover — sell a platform everyone files under mood boards. The tonal left turn is the whole memory.

Steal it An unexpected genre earns more attention than one more tasteful grid.
