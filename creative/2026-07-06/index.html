The First Cut

The work worth stealing from this week — shown, with a short take on why each one lands.

This week's picks

Columbia Sportswear

Expedition Impossible

Columbia offered its entire company — mannequins, coffee machine, a taxidermy beaver — to any flat-earther who could photograph the edge of the world. The flex is the message: a brand so sure its gear survives the ends of the earth it'll wager the office on it. Steal it: let the product's promise write the dare.

Heinz · VML

Is That Heinz?

Restaurants were refilling Heinz bottles with cheaper ketchup, so Heinz added a border in its exact Pantone red — hold the sauce against the label and a fake gives itself away; non-Heinz refills reportedly fell 73%. Steal it: a brand asset can be proof, not just decoration.

See the work →

Club Deportivo Municipal · McCann Lima

The Thousand Sponsors of Muni

A cash-strapped Peruvian club cut its shirt into 1,000 pixel-grid sponsor squares from ~100 soles each, turning the kit into a neighborhood directory any local business could join (Cannes Sport Grand Prix). Steal it: sometimes a thousand tiny doors beat one big one.

See the work →

SAP · BBDO

Office Cat

SAP explains its business AI through a Van Gogh–styled cat that pads around an office naming the dull, useful things the software actually handles. It makes an abstract enterprise capability legible and even charming — the hardest trick in B2B. Steal it: when the product is invisible, borrow a character people already love.

See the work →

Dell · EssenceMediacom

I.T. Squad

Dell built a comedy series about two IT guys solving problems pulled straight from real Reddit threads, and ran it native on Reddit instead of dragging a skeptical audience to a landing page. Steal it: match the platform's texture and the ad stops reading as an ad.

See the work →

Pinterest · House of Creative

The P is for Performance

Pinterest went full grindhouse — mini action films with two deadpan heroines and a "High action! Lower funnel!" voiceover — to sell a platform everyone files under mood boards. The tonal left turn is the whole memory. Steal it: an unexpected genre earns more attention than one more tasteful grid.
