The First Cut

The work worth stealing from this week. Shown, with a short take on why each one lands.

This week's picks

Columbia Sportswear

Expedition Impossible

Agency
—
Motivation
Prove the gear's toughness by betting the whole company on it.
Emotion
Bravado, absurdist humor, confidence.
Type
Utility expedition-label sans.
Palette
Storm navy · flag red · bone

Columbia offered its entire company, mannequins, coffee machine, a taxidermy beaver, to any flat-earther who could photograph the edge of the world. The dare is the message. A brand this sure its gear survives the ends of the earth will bet the office on it. Steal it: let the product's promise write the dare.

Club Deportivo Municipal

The Thousand Sponsors of Muni

Agency
McCann Lima
Motivation
Turn a funding crisis into a fame-driving, community-owned idea.
Emotion
Civic pride, underdog solidarity, belonging.
Type
Sport-kit numerals, condensed grotesque.
Palette
Ink · white pixel grid

A cash-strapped Peruvian club cut its shirt into 1,000 pixel-grid sponsor squares, ~100 soles each, and turned the kit into a neighborhood directory any local business could join (Cannes Sport Grand Prix). Steal it: sometimes a thousand small doors beat one big one.

SAP

Office Cat

Agency
BBDO
Motivation
Cut through AI hype by making enterprise AI concrete and disarming.
Emotion
Charm, curiosity, relief.
Type
Clean corporate grotesque.
Palette
Van Gogh blue · gold

SAP explains its business AI through a cat painted like a Van Gogh, padding around an office and naming the dull, useful things the software actually handles. It makes an abstract enterprise capability clear and even charming, the hardest trick in B2B. Steal it: when the product is invisible, borrow a character people already love.

Pinterest

The P is for Performance

Agency
House of Creative · dir. Tim Godsall
Motivation
Reframe a mood-board app as a serious performance platform.
Emotion
Delight, surprise, tongue-in-cheek adrenaline.
Type
Grindhouse display titles, chunky and distressed.
Palette
Pinterest red · ink · action gold

Pinterest went full grindhouse to sell a platform everyone files under mood boards. The films run as mini action movies with two deadpan heroines and a "High action! Lower funnel!" voiceover. The tonal left turn is the whole memory. Steal it: an unexpected genre earns more attention than one more tasteful grid.
