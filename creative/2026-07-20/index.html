Show Your Work

Four pieces betting on craft over the hard sell — a stereotype reclaimed, a mother's life wagered, a yellow frame left empty, and AI's hardest questions asked out loud.

This week's picks

Castle Milk Stout · Joe Public

This Is African Time

Agency
Joe Public (dir. Zee Ntuli, Darling Films)
Motivation
Reclaim a self-inflicted stereotype — "African time" as presence, not lateness
Emotion
Unhurried pride, calm
Type
Brand film
Palette
Stout black, bone, amber

Built from 300-plus stills, so the craft is the argument: the frantic cut of modern life set against the calm it skips past. Steal the channel discipline — radio spots cut off mid-story and finish on Spotify, turning the medium itself into the message about stolen time.

Back Market · Mother

Swear On Our Mom's Life

Agency
Mother (dir. Harold Einstein, Packer)
Motivation
Kill the "second-hand means second-best" doubt around refurbished tech
Emotion
Deadpan absurdity, warmth
Type
Social / comedy film
Palette
Clinical white, ink, mint

The barrier is trust, so the founder literally wagers his real mother's estate — down to a novelty cow-shaped egg cup. Absurd scale makes a dull guarantee impossible to forget. Steal: when the truth is real but boring, bet everything on it, out loud.

National Geographic · Terri & Sandy

See Things You Could Only Imagine

Agency
Terri & Sandy
Motivation
Launch the Museum of Exploration by making D.C. itself an extension of it
Emotion
Curiosity, wonder
Type
OOH / print / design
Palette
Nat Geo yellow, ink, paper

The most-owned frame in publishing, used as empty space — a bus-length yellow stripe is the Spinosaurus. Restraint as flex. Steal: your most recognizable asset can do more as a container than as a stamp.

Anthropic (Claude) · Mother

There's Hope In Hard Questions

Agency
Mother
Motivation
Own AI's harms out loud to claim the responsible-actor high ground
Emotion
Divisive — sober to some, ominous to many
Type
Brand film (tech)
Palette
Smoke, slate, ash

The week's most-argued-about ad — worth watching because it split the room. Owning your industry's sins to claim the moral high ground is a proven play, but tone is everything: one shade too dark and conviction reads as dread. Reception was sharply divided, Sam Altman pile-on included.
