Scroll down. A string of rings along a light axis slides together and settles into the offset contour stack in the corner.

What's happening

Rings of varied size sit strung along a diagonal, like the artifacts of a lens flare. As you scroll they travel down the axis, resizing as they go, until they take their slots in the drifting offset contours — flare resolving to the mark.

Keep scrolling — they converge at the end.

Why it matters

Flare is scatter; focus is signal. The motion is the whole thesis of the brief — pulling a diffuse field into one clear point.

How it behaves

Each ring interpolates its center along the axis to its home in the offset stack and its radius to that ring's size, eased, with opacity rising. By the end the flare has become the Offset mark.
