Totality

Scroll down. The eclipse shrinks into the corner — and at the end, the whole page flips to a negative.

What's happening

The page reads normally on light paper. As you reach the bottom, the ink and paper swap: background goes dark, text and rules go light, and the corner mark becomes its reversed self — the moment of totality.

Keep scrolling — the flip lands in the final stretch.

Why it matters

It's a bold, one-note gag: a full-page negative timed to totality. Probably too much for a working digest — but worth seeing once.

How it behaves

Two CSS variables, --ink and --paper, interpolate past 85% scroll. Everything drawn from those tokens inverts at once — no per-element recoloring.
