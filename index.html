Item 01SEO

Google's May 2026 Core Update lands mid-rollout — every client site is on the table.

What's happening

Google released the May 2026 Core Update on Thursday, May 21 at 08:40 PT. The rollout could take up to two weeks — meaning it's still finishing this week and into early June. Coverage is broad: every industry, every language. Pages flagged as weak in usefulness, originality, author trust, or intent match are the ones getting reshuffled. The timing — two days after the Google I/O AI search keynote — has SEO professionals reading the update as an AI-era trust signal calibration.

Why it matters

Core updates aren't AI overlays — they reweight everything that already exists. Sites with thin pages, unclear authorship, or stale technical content can drop without warning. Recovery requires real content changes, not patches.

Apply to your clients
OGI

Pull rankings for the catalog spec pages and key product family pages (ALGRIP®, OnGrip®, Wheels n' Heels®, Grater Access®) on June 1–3, once the rollout settles. Where pages are losing position, the fix is almost always the same: thin H1+image+spec-table pages need supporting prose — load specs, application context, distributor-relevant detail. This dovetails with the existing "more digital surface area" need.

CBS

Monitor the operational excellence service pages and any individual consultant bios. If service pages move, the gap is almost always author/E-E-A-T signals (no named author, no practitioner credentials, no last-updated stamp). Add a named CBS senior advisor as the byline on each service page and stamp a visible "Last reviewed [date]" line.

BCV

Check the assisted living / memory care / pricing pages and any "questions families ask" content. Family-decision-journey pages are exactly what Google rewards when written by real staff with credentials and demotes when they read generic. If pages drop, the fix is named clinical/admissions author + clear last-updated.

Vinyl

The agency's own service pages and case studies need the same audit. Cases without named clients (where allowed) and without dated outcomes are the most exposed.

Item 02AI Search

Google AI Overviews adds Preferred Sources + "Expert Advice" block.

What's happening

On May 27, Google extended its Preferred Sources feature: any site publishing fresh content is now eligible to be cited inside AI Overviews and AI Mode, and users see a "Subscribed" label when an answer pulls from a publication they follow. Google says early testing shows people are "significantly more likely" to click through when that label appears. This builds on the May 6 AIO structural update that added inline citations next to the specific text they support, hover previews showing site names, and a new "Expert Advice" block that pulls first-hand perspectives from forums, social media, and review sites.

Why it matters

The unit of competition in Google search is no longer "the page that ranks." It's "the source AI cites." Preferred Sources also surfaces a UX behavior worth designing for: when a user has subscribed to or followed a brand, that brand gets visible credit inside the AI answer. That makes email/Substack/LinkedIn followership a flywheel for AI search visibility, not just an email list.

Apply to your clients
CBS

The "Expert Advice" block pulls from forums, Reddit, and review sites. CBS's COO/plant manager audience lives on a few specific subreddits (r/manufacturing, r/lean, r/operationsmanagement) and a handful of LinkedIn groups. A 4-week test: have one CBS senior advisor commit to 2 substantive answers/week in those threads, each linked to a CBS resource. Goal isn't direct traffic — it's becoming a source AIO cites for operational excellence queries.

OGI

Engineering forums (Eng-Tips, r/StructuralEngineering, AISC discussions) are where AI Overviews are now sourcing technical "expert advice." Plan: identify the 25 highest-volume queries around metal grating spec selection, get 3 OGI engineers participating substantively in those threads with profile links back to the OGI catalog. This is the manufacturing equivalent of E-E-A-T.

IF

For pre-launch, Preferred Sources favors content from publications people actively follow. The pre-launch playbook gets sharper: drive nonprofit Executive Directors (the Natalie persona) to subscribe to the IF newsletter NOW. The bigger the subscribed base, the more often IF gets the "Subscribed" label inside AIO when ED-stage queries are asked. Reframe the founding-member waitlist landing page to lead with the newsletter sub, then the waitlist.

BCV

Caring.com, A Place for Mom community reviews, and senior care Facebook groups are exactly the "expert advice" sources AIO now surfaces. Schedule current resident families to write community reviews (with permission) in the next 60 days while the brand video shoot is in production — the social proof compounds with the video assets.

Item 03AI Search

ChatGPT's branded-link change is sustaining — referrals up ~60% and holding.

What's happening

The May 7, 2026 Branded Link Update inside ChatGPT (brand names now show as clickable callouts inside answers, not just citations at the bottom) is not reverting. Three weeks in, Similarweb data shows total ChatGPT referrals up 157.7% week-over-week vs. the pre-change baseline, with homepage referrals up 354.7%. The share of ChatGPT answers carrying a brand link jumped from ~0.4% to ~6.2% — roughly 14x. Across the months that followed, the lift has held in the ~60% range, and ChatGPT referral traffic converts at 7.1% — second only to paid search.

Why it matters

For a year, the "AI search is a traffic sink" frame was correct. It is now provably wrong for branded queries. ChatGPT is acting like a referral source. If your brand is mentioned but not linked inside a ChatGPT answer, you are leaving meaningful click-through behind.

Apply to your clients
IF

IF's positioning ("AI-native nonprofit fundraising membership," Natalie persona, 20+ systems) is exactly the category-defining brand language ChatGPT can attach to a clickable homepage. Action: in the next 30 days, ensure IF's homepage and "what is Integrated Fundraising" page are crawlable to ChatGPT (allow GPTBot, don't block in robots.txt) AND that brand language is consistent across the website, LinkedIn, podcast appearances, and guest posts. Test monthly: "What is Integrated Fundraising?" — check for the inline link.

OGI

Branded-name traffic to ohiogratings.com is a measurable upside here because the product trademarks (ALGRIP®, OnGrip®, Wheels n' Heels®, Grater Access®) are unique enough that ChatGPT can connect them cleanly. Audit: ask ChatGPT "What is ALGRIP grating?" and "Who makes OnGrip stair treads?" — confirm OHGratings is the linked brand. If not, the fix is brand mention frequency on third-party engineering/spec sites.

CBS

CBS shows up in ChatGPT for operational excellence queries when there's clean brand language across LinkedIn, the blog, and conference content. Test 8 query variants ("best operational excellence consultants for mid-market manufacturers," "PE-backed operational consulting firms") — wherever CBS shows up unlinked, that's the gap to close with brand-mention-density work over Q3.

Item 04Paid Social

LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads are quietly outperforming everything else.

What's happening

2026 LinkedIn benchmarks just landed and the gap is wider than expected: Thought Leader Ads (sponsoring a real person's post) are averaging 4.65% CTR vs. 0.68% for every other format — a 6.9x lift. CPC is $0.51 vs. $2.42. CPL is running 40–50% lower than Sponsored Content. The algorithm shift backs this up: LinkedIn moved to relevance-based distribution earlier in 2026, posts with off-platform links are penalized ~60%, and personal profiles generate 8x more engagement than company pages.

Why it matters

The cost of acquiring a B2B click via a real human's profile on LinkedIn is now roughly one-fifth the cost of acquiring one via a company page. For B2B service brands with a credible practitioner-leader voice, this is the single biggest paid-channel arbitrage available in 2026.

Apply to your clients
CBS

Run a 4-week Thought Leader Ad test promoting the next CBS operational excellence article from a CBS senior advisor's profile, targeting COOs and VPs of Operations at $100M–$500M revenue manufacturers in PE portfolios. Budget: $4–6K. Benchmark to beat: 4% CTR, $1.50 CPC. If the test hits, scale to 2 TLAs/month with rotating CBS leaders for the rest of 2026.

IF

IF is founder-led and pre-launch — the ideal TLA setup. Run a 6-week TLA series from the IF founder's profile promoting the founding-member waitlist, targeting Executive Directors and Development Directors at nonprofits $1M–$25M in annual revenue. Pair with the bold/anti-grind/system-thinking voice — TLAs reward strong POV, not "5 tips" content.

Vinyl

Eat your own dog food. Stand up a TLA from Josh's profile promoting a Vinyl case study (B2B or nonprofit), targeting marketing directors and CMOs at mid-market B2B/nonprofit organizations. Use Vinyl's results as the proof point in the next agency pitch.

OGI

Likely a longer-term play. OGI's audience (structural engineers, architects, procurement) is on LinkedIn but skews older and less platform-native. Recommend running one targeted TLA experiment from an OGI engineering lead's profile around a technical spec topic (e.g., load ratings for ALGRIP®), but treat results as exploratory, not a primary channel.

Item 05AI Agents

AI agent platforms went from demo to shipping this month.

What's happening

The last two weeks saw a wave of agent platform launches with real B2B utility, not demo videos: Madhive's Maverick AI Agents for local advertising placement; Alteryx's Agent Studio + MCP Server (turns data workflows into autonomous agents without IT); Attentive's agentic AI for messaging at its Thread 2026 event; and Figma's in-canvas AI assistant that runs multiple agents simultaneously. Gartner now projects 40% of enterprise applications will ship task-specific AI agents by end of 2026, up from less than 5% last year.

Why it matters

The "AI in marketing" story has shifted from generation (writing copy, making images) to orchestration (agents doing multi-step work). For agencies and operators, the agents that win are not horizontal "do everything" tools — they're vertical and tied to a workflow. This is a positioning opportunity for both Vinyl and IF.

Apply to your clients
IF

IF's "20+ AI systems for nonprofits" positioning lines up exactly with this shift. The press around Madhive/Alteryx/Attentive's vertical agent launches is air cover for the IF launch story: "Generic AI assistants aren't built for fundraising. IF is." Clip 1–2 of these announcements and quote them in the founding-member positioning email sequence as "this is the moment vertical AI is winning."

Vinyl

Vinyl could position itself as the agency that pairs clients with the right vertical AI agent platform (rather than building bespoke automations from scratch every time). Quick deliverable: a 1-page "AI Agent Platform Map for B2B/Nonprofit" mapping the new agent platforms to common Vinyl client use cases. Use it as a partner-led growth lead magnet.

CBS

CBS's manufacturing audience cares about operational AI, not marketing AI. A longer-term content thread for CBS: "What manufacturing leaders should know about agentic AI on the plant floor" — explicitly NOT a marketing piece, but an operational excellence piece, which is exactly the CBS voice.

Item 06Email

Email deliverability enforcement is biting — 30% of bulk senders still non-compliant.

What's happening

Gmail, Yahoo, and Microsoft have moved from "we'll warn you" to "we'll quietly route you to spam" in 2026. The original 2024 bulk sender requirements (SPF + DKIM + DMARC, one-click unsubscribe, <0.30% spam rate) are now enforced harder: non-compliant bulk senders are seeing spam-folder delivery jump from 5–10% to 22–34%, and roughly 30% of senders remain partially non-compliant on at least one requirement. The 0.30% spam-complaint threshold is unforgiving — once tripped, the domain is ineligible for delivery remediation until the rate stays below for 7 consecutive days.

Why it matters

Deliverability has become an infrastructure problem disguised as a marketing problem. For any client doing cold outreach or large nurture sends, an unaudited DKIM record or a soft DMARC policy is now a measurable revenue leak.

Apply to your clients
IF

Pre-launch cold email is core to the IF growth model. Before the launch send hits scale, audit: (1) SPF, DKIM, DMARC published and aligned for the IF root domain and any subdomain used for sending; (2) DMARC at p=quarantine minimum (not p=none) — required for credibility at Gmail/Yahoo; (3) RFC 8058 one-click unsubscribe in every cold email template. Run a deliverability test send to a Gmail/Yahoo/Outlook test panel two weeks before the founding-member open.

CBS

Cold outreach to COOs and PE operating partners is part of the CBS top-of-funnel. Same audit applies. CBS's risk is sending from a sales platform on a subdomain that may not have DKIM properly aligned with the sending domain. A 1-day audit pays for itself.

Vinyl

Vinyl runs email for clients — this is a billable audit offer. Package as a "Q3 Deliverability Health Check" — 2-hour DNS/auth audit, 2-hour list-hygiene review, deliverable is a fix list. Easy add-on to existing retainers.
