01

AI-search monitoring became a real product category — and an agency service

What's happening

On July 3, Profound launched "Aim," an always-on background agent that continuously monitors AI-search signals — brand visibility, prompt trends, citations, sentiment, and competitive benchmarks across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity — and turns them into prioritized project briefs. The same week, agency Avenue Z announced it had become a Profound Agency Partner, packaging AI-visibility measurement as a client service. How a brand shows up in AI answers moved from a guess to something you can instrument and report on.

Why it matters

AEO is shifting from a content tactic to a monitored channel with tooling, benchmarks, and a reporting cadence — the same maturation SEO went through a decade ago.

Vinyl

Stand up a monthly "AI Visibility Report" as a productized add-on. Even without a paid tool, run a fixed prompt set (e.g., 15 buyer questions per client) through ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity monthly, log whether the client is cited and how they're described, and deliver a one-page trend. A low-lift retainer expansion that also generates case-study proof.

CBS

Build the tracked prompt set around operational-excellence buyer questions ("how to reduce unplanned downtime in a mid-market plant," "best operational excellence consultants for PE-backed manufacturers") and check monthly whether CBS is named. Where it isn't, that's the content brief.

OGI

Track spec-stage prompts ("load-rated bar grating for pedestrian walkway," "ADA-compliant metal grating," "ALGRIP anti-slip grating specs") to see whether OGI surfaces when engineers ask AI tools before pulling a catalog.

What's happening

Recent AEO data shows ~83% of AI citations on commercial/evaluation queries come from pages updated within the last 12 months (60%+ within six months), and the overlap between Google's organic top-10 and what AI Overviews actually cite has weakened from ~76% in mid-2025 to somewhere between 17% and 54% in early 2026. Google's new AI-search guidance frames AEO/GEO as "still SEO," but the practical mechanic has changed: structure, freshness, and credible sourcing are the three levers that most reliably predict whether a page gets quoted.

Why it matters

A page that ranked #1 for two years can now be invisible inside the AI answer a buyer actually reads. Update cadence is now a ranking factor for citations.

CBS

Add a "last reviewed" refresh pass to the top 10–15 revenue pages and cornerstone thought-leadership articles — restate the core answer in the first 100 words, add a dated stat, and stamp the update. Fold this into the existing weekly content pipeline rather than treating it as new work.

OGI

The catalog is the strength but likely reads as static to an AI crawler. Prioritize refreshing the highest-intent product/spec pages with dated, extractable facts (load ratings, finish options, compliance notes) so they read as current authoritative sources.

IF

For the pre-launch content/SEO push, build the founding-member content as clean answer pages to 10–25 real ED/Development-Director questions, and set a standing monthly refresh so IF stays inside the citation window while the category is still forming.

BCV

Refresh the core "how to choose senior living / levels of care / cost" pages with current, dated specifics so families using AI tools get accurate, up-to-date answers that name BCV.

A cluster of July-3 deals made the pattern explicit — Zoom agreed to acquire Common Room (a buyer-intelligence platform unifying first-party CRM, product, and engagement data), and the week's analysis argued the AI model itself is commoditizing while the data and decisioning layers beneath it become the real differentiator. LinkedIn now rewards relevance and native text — carousels and external links are penalized

−60%
reach penalty on posts with external links
~5% vs ~65%

What's happening

LinkedIn's 2026 distribution overhaul (its "Generative Recommenders" system) shifted reach from follower count to topical relevance. Reported effects: native text posts outperforming carousels for B2B organic reach (one analysis of 147 B2B accounts found native posts averaged ~28% higher reach and ~34% better engagement), a "depth score" now weighting dwell time / comment quality over raw likes, and external links cutting reach by ~60%. Company pages get ~5% of feed allocation versus ~65% for personal profiles.

Why it matters

Consistent topical posting from individual experts now builds the "topical credibility" the algorithm rewards — and the link-in-first-comment / native-content playbook matters more than ever.

CBS

Shift executive LinkedIn from carousel/graphic posts to native text posts (native document/PDF only where it earns dwell time), publish consistently from CBS practitioners' personal profiles rather than the company page, and put article links in the first comment. Anchor every post to a narrow topic set (operational excellence, plant performance) so the algorithm associates the accounts with those themes.

Vinyl

Same play for Vinyl's own thought-leadership: partner-led growth means posting from people, not the brand page, on a tight topical lane. Update any internal LinkedIn playbook to reflect the external-link reach penalty.

IF

The founder-led, anti-grind voice is perfect for native text posts. Lean into short, opinionated, story-first posts from the founder's personal profile to build topical authority in the nonprofit-fundraising lane ahead of launch — no carousels needed.

06

Hard email enforcement at Gmail / Yahoo / Microsoft threatens cold-outreach programs

0.10%
spam-complaint ceiling for bulk senders
SPF · DKIM · DMARC
all now required — failures are permanently rejected, not deferred

What's happening

As of late 2025 and enforced through 2026, Gmail, Yahoo, and Microsoft moved from temporary deferrals to permanent rejection for mail that fails authentication. SPF, DKIM, and DMARC are all now required (a syntactically clean SPF, not just any SPF), one-click unsubscribe (RFC 8058) is mandatory for bulk senders, and the spam-complaint ceiling is ~0.10%. Microsoft's enforcement hit B2B hardest because it hosts most corporate inboxes.

Why it matters

Any program that leans on cold or bulk email is now one authentication gap away from silent, permanent rejection — not the spam folder, but never delivered.

IF

Cold email is a core pre-launch channel for reaching EDs and Development Directors. Before scaling the founding-member outreach, verify SPF/DKIM/DMARC are correctly configured on the sending domain(s), warm the domain, keep one-click unsubscribe in place, and watch the complaint rate against the 0.10% ceiling. A dedicated sending subdomain protects the primary domain's reputation. [Flag: confirm current sending-domain setup before scaling — treat as a pre-launch checklist item.]

CBS

Cold outreach to manufacturing executives runs straight into Microsoft-hosted corporate inboxes, the most affected environment. Audit CBS's sending-domain authentication and reputation now; deliverability, not copy, is likely the first bottleneck for the outbound program.

What's happening

2026 senior-living research shows families increasingly start with an AI tool — plugging in emotional questions, comparing communities, and verifying trust before ever hitting a community's site — with traditional search volume projected to drop ~25% by year-end. Meanwhile a BILD & Co. survey found 92% of web inquiries go unanswered within 24 hours and 80% go unanswered entirely. The decision-maker is usually an adult child (increasingly Gen X) under emotional and time pressure.

Why it matters

For senior living, two things now decide the win: whether AI tools surface and trust your community, and whether a stressed family gets a fast human response. Neither conflicts with BCV's in-flight video work — they complement it.

BCV

(1) Make sure the pages an AI tool reads to answer "best senior living near [area]," "cost of assisted living," and "memory care vs. assisted living" are current, specific, and name BCV with trust signals (accreditations, reviews, pricing transparency). (2) Audit inbound-inquiry response time — if BCV is anywhere near the 92% / 24-hour industry gap, a faster response workflow may move more families than any new ad spend. The brand video in production is the right emotional asset for the adult-child decision-maker; this is about making sure families find it and get a fast reply, not about changing the video direction.
