01

Quoted, Not Clicked

The click stopped being the prize this week: AI answers cite their sources instead of sending traffic, ads moved onto AI surfaces, and the brands that win are the ones getting quoted.

TL;DR

  • 01AI became ad infrastructure. Google, Meta, and OpenAI spent the week turning AI from a tool into the operating layer of advertising - AI Mode crossed a billion queries a month, ChatGPT's ad pilot widened, and Meta's Andromeda rewarded creative variety over targeting. Every 2026 plan now needs a stance on AI surfaces, not just AI tools.
  • 02Clicks are collapsing; citation is the currency. 93% of AI Mode queries end in zero clicks and AI-Overview queries cut organic CTR by roughly 61% - but pages cited inside the answer see about a 35% click lift. Getting cited inside the answer now matters more than winning the blue link.
  • 03Personal beats corporate, again. LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads run about 2.68% CTR versus 0.42% for a single image, at roughly 77% lower cost per click, and the new interest-graph algorithm amplifies expert-led content. The cheapest pipeline routes through a real person's profile.
01

AI Mode hits a billion queries - citation is the new ranking

93%
of AI Mode queries end in zero clicks
+35%
click lift for pages cited inside the answer vs. uncited top-10

What's happening

Google AI Mode now handles over a billion queries a month and 75 million daily users - one of the fastest adoption curves in its history - and on April 29 the Android search bar quietly became "Ask Google." An analysis of 25.1M impressions found 93% of AI Mode queries get zero clicks, and organic CTR on AI-Overview queries fell from 1.76% to 0.61%.

Why it matters

The traffic side of informational SEO is permanently lower. But pages cited inside the answer see roughly a 35% click lift over uncited top-10 results - so the goal moves from ranking to being quoted.

How to act

Audit your fifteen highest-intent pages against AI Overviews and Perplexity. Wherever a competitor is cited and you aren't, rewrite with a 40-60 word answer-first lead that answers the query outright, then an attributable stat or expert quote - the shape AI lifts cleanly. Stop optimizing to rank; optimize to be quoted.

02

A third ad ecosystem is forming - and it's agentic

What's happening

OpenAI's ChatGPT Atlas browser - Chromium-based, with ChatGPT embedded in every window - is rolling out to Windows and mobile, and OpenAI's self-serve Ads Manager pilot widened so small US businesses can upload creative and set budgets, against a projected $2.5B in 2026 ad revenue. New voice models translating 70 languages shipped alongside it.

Why it matters

A third ad ecosystem is forming next to Google and Meta - and unlike them it's agentic: the "user" making the buying decision is increasingly an AI acting on someone's behalf. Whoever builds structured, machine-readable content now becomes the default the agent picks later.

How to act

Get on the ChatGPT Ads waitlist and run a small pilot the week access opens - the platform-quirks learning is worth more than the spend. More important: structure your content so that when someone tells their browser "find me an X that isn't a sales demo," you're a candidate. Structure now, default later.

03

The cheapest B2B pipeline runs through a person, not a page

2.68% vs 0.42%
Thought Leader Ad CTR vs. single-image ad CTR
-77%
cost per click on Thought Leader Ads

What's happening

LinkedIn fully rolled out 360Brew, an LLM ranking system that replaced the follow-graph with an interest-graph - content now spreads by what you engage with, not who you follow - amplifying expert frameworks and suppressing pods and clickbait. Thought Leader Ads (sponsoring a real person's post) post about 2.68% median CTR versus 0.42% for single-image ads, roughly $2.29 CPC, and 77% lower cost per click; 60% of B2B buyers now meet a brand through creator content before they ever fill out a form.

Why it matters

For B2B, the lowest-cost route to pipeline runs through a real person's profile, amplified by Thought Leader Ads. The company-page model is the worst of both worlds: low organic reach and 4-6x worse paid economics.

How to act

Pick two practitioner posts on a specific framework and put a modest Thought Leader Ad behind them, tightly targeted. Shift organic to 60-120 second talking-head videos on real, numbered ideas - one a week from a named expert. Let the logo sit this one out.

04

AEO grows up - write for every engine, and keep it fresh

83%
of AI citations come from pages updated in the last 12 months

What's happening

Answer-engine optimization hardened into a real playbook. For commercial queries, 83% of AI citations come from pages updated in the last 12 months (60%+ within six). Engines differ: AI Overviews pull snippable structure off the top-10; Perplexity rewards freshness plus authority; Claude prefers long comprehensive guides; Gemini reads multimodal. "Rank #1" is giving way to AI-visibility and citation-rate metrics.

Why it matters

SEO and AEO are no longer one workflow. You can rank #1 and be invisible in the answer if your structure is wrong, and get cited heavily by Perplexity while sitting on Google's page two. The teams that win organize for multi-engine pickup.

How to act

Write the important topics twice - a short answer-first version with snippable subheads for AI Overviews, and a long comprehensive guide for Perplexity and Claude - and add a quarterly freshness pass (a new stat, a dated update) to anything over six months old. Two shapes, one topic.

05

A partial email setup is now a failure

0.10%
hard spam-complaint ceiling for bulk senders
22-34%
spam-folder rate for non-compliant senders, up from 5-10%

What's happening

Gmail and Yahoo escalated bulk-sender enforcement: mail that fails authentication is now rejected, not spam-foldered. SPF, DKIM, and DMARC are all required above 5,000 messages a day, one-click unsubscribe must process within 48 hours, and the spam-complaint ceiling is a hard 0.10%. Two years in, about 30% of bulk senders are still partially non-compliant - and they see spam-folder rates jump from 5-10% to 22-34%.

Why it matters

"Set it and forget it" email auth is now an active deliverability risk. Run cold outreach, lifecycle email, or any list over 5K to consumer inboxes and you can lose a third of your inbox placement without changing a single setting.

How to act

Audit now: DMARC at p=quarantine or reject, DKIM signing every sending domain, a clean SPF, one-click unsubscribe live, complaint rate under 0.10%. Send cold or bulk from a warmed subdomain so a stumble never poisons your root domain. It's plumbing - but plumbing decides whether the work you already did ever arrives.

06

Real people, real prices, real humans - the trust reset

What's happening

2026 research on high-consideration, family-decision categories converged on three shifts: real people on camera beat stock lifestyle content; buyers want explicit cost and "what's included" on the site before they'll call; and the AI-research overload is paradoxically pushing people back toward human contact - calls, tours, a named person. Sales and marketing are dissolving into one continuous experience from first search to first call.

Why it matters

For any considered, high-trust purchase, the same thing now wins: show the real people, publish the numbers buyers are hunting for, and make it easy to reach a human. It complements brand and creative work - it doesn't replace it.

How to act

Put real customers and staff on camera, not actors. Publish a cost / what's-included page written for the person doing late-night research, plus a "what to ask when you compare" checklist. And make human follow-up fast and obvious - when someone's overwhelmed, a real reply wins the decision.
