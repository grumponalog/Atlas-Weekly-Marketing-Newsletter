Hands Off the Wheel

What actually moved in marketing the week of May 18-25 - Google handed campaigns to agents, a core update reweighted the web, and authenticity kept beating polish. And how to act on each.

Executive summary — TL;DR

  • 01A faster, Gemini-powered core update reweighted the web. Google's May 2026 core update rolled out on a six-week cadence - twice as fast as recent years - rewarding original, people-first content and exposing thin, recycled, or AI-padded pages. “Set it and forget it” content now decays faster.
  • 02Google handed the ad stack to agents. Google Marketing Live reframed everything around agents - a cross-product Gemini advisor, in-ad conversational lead capture, and agentic-commerce plumbing. Campaign decisions, and even the transaction, are moving inside the ad.
  • 03The paid AI channel opened to everyone. OpenAI dropped ChatGPT Ads' $50K minimum and added CPC bidding - a third ad ecosystem now testable at small-business budgets. No benchmarks yet, so treat the first month as learning, not performance.
01

A faster, Gemini-powered core update reweights the web

~6 weeks
between core updates now - roughly half the recent cadence

What's happening

Google confirmed the May 2026 core update on May 21 - a roughly two-week rollout and the second of the year, only six weeks after March. It's positioned as the next generation of ranking, powered by Gemini quality models, explicitly rewarding original, helpful, people-first content and demoting automated, ad-bloated pages.

Why it matters

A faster cadence makes SEO a reactive discipline - static content decays faster, and pages leaning on aggregated, recycled, or AI-thin writing are more exposed than they were six months ago.

How to act

Don't panic-edit mid-rollout. A week after it completes, pull a movers list and look for pages that should rank but slipped - that's the model reading them as thin, not a technical fault. The fix is genuine, authored substance: on catalog-thin or spec pages, add 150-250 words of real practitioner context (“why experts choose this,” field notes) before the next update lands.

02

Google hands the ad stack to agents

What's happening

Google Marketing Live was the biggest Ads change since Performance Max: Ask Advisor, a cross-product Gemini agent spanning Ads, Analytics, and Merchant Center with shared memory; Business Agent for Leads, conversational AI inside the ad unit that answers a prospect's questions and captures the lead; and formal agentic-commerce infrastructure so buyers can transact inside Search.

Why it matters

Google is committing to a future where agents - not human managers - make most campaign decisions, and buyers transact without leaving the results page. The near-term prize for lead-gen is the in-ad agent: it collapses “click, landing page, form, reply” into “ask in the ad, get answered, book.”

How to act

Pilot the in-ad lead agent the moment it reaches your vertical - a search campaign where the agent answers the qualifying question (“do you work with X?”) before the click. Turn on the cross-product advisor for faster insight. Treat it as a test this quarter, not a rip-and-replace.

03

The $50K door comes off - ChatGPT ads open to everyone

What's happening

OpenAI opened ChatGPT's self-serve Ads Manager: the $50,000 minimum is gone and CPC bidding is in, with agency and ad-tech partners lined up (Dentsu, Publicis, WPP, Adobe, Criteo, StackAdapt). Against 800M weekly users, small-budget B2B and nonprofit experiments are now feasible.

Why it matters

A third ad ecosystem is now testable at small-business budgets - but there are no real benchmarks yet. Anyone going in should treat the first 30 days as learning spend, not performance spend.

How to act

Run a small pilot on a tight, high-intent query set tied to a landing page you already trust - and cap it as a learning budget. The real payoff is a month of platform-quirks knowledge you can turn into a capability, plus early inbound from buyers already searching the AI surface.

04

Wikipedia and Reddit run the AI answer - your press release doesn't

25%+
of US ChatGPT citations come from Wikipedia + Reddit
Top 20
WSJ, NYT, and Bloomberg don't crack it

What's happening

5W's Q1 2026 citation audit, synthesizing nine datasets across hundreds of millions of LLM citations, found Wikipedia (13.15%) and Reddit (11.97%) together drive over a quarter of US ChatGPT citations - while WSJ, NYT, and Bloomberg don't appear in the top 20. Engines differ (Perplexity leans LinkedIn, NIH, G2; Gemini behaves more like classic SEO), and it moves fast: ChatGPT's Reddit share swung from ~60% to ~10% in six weeks.

Why it matters

The tier-one-PR playbook - land in WSJ, get cited everywhere - is broken for AI surfaces. Visibility now comes from entity consistency, presence on high-authority community sites, and fresh, well-structured pages on your own domain. Because the rankings move, it's a tracked metric, not a one-time project.

How to act

Find where your buyers actually ask - the two or three communities for your category - and show up there credibly, as a real named person, not a brand voice: helpful, non-promotional answers a few times a week. Keep your own highest-intent pages fresh (83% of cited pages were updated in the last year). Don't fabricate; earn the mention.

05

LinkedIn starts scoring depth - and taxing link-drops

~60%
reach penalty on posts with external links

What's happening

LinkedIn's 2026 LLM-based ranking keeps rippling. Confirmed now: a Depth Score built on dwell time and meaningful engagement (not reactions) is the headline input; external links cut reach ~60%; engagement bait is suppressed; recycled thought leadership gets downranked; and company pages get ~5% of feed allocation versus ~65% for personal profiles.

Why it matters

Two things are now mathematically true, not just stylistic: publish from a person's profile, not the company page - and the old “repost the blog with thoughts?” play is dead. Posts have to earn dwell time.

How to act

Move distribution to named people's profiles and write native - a document carousel or a text breakdown with a real point of view, link in the first comment if at all. Aim for dwell time and genuine comments, not likes. Original framing beats polished restating.

06

Phone-shot beats produced - authenticity is the format

What's happening

Senior-living marketing research converged on a shift with wide reach: short, phone-shot content showing real people and behind-the-scenes moments is outperforming polished, staged production. Buyers trust authenticity over production value, and Meta, YouTube, and search reward the same. Speed-to-response stays a top conversion lever - minutes beat hours by a wide margin.

Why it matters

For considered, human purchases this is a “yes, and”: a hero brand video is still the right anchor, but the supporting stream around it should look candid, not produced. Real beats polished.

How to act

Keep the hero video, and layer a parallel cadence of short, unedited, vertical clips from inside the real thing - a genuine moment, no production. Hand one or two people a simple five-clips-a-week prompt list. And tighten response time: when someone reaches out, a fast human reply is the cheapest edge you have.
